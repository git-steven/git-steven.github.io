## ⭐ Where Signal Is Extracted

Between **Data → Information** the following *transformations* happen:

- Validation & cleaning of raw values  
- Filtering out noise and non-relevant bits  
- Structuring and contextualizing streams of measurements  
- Adding description (who, what, where, when)  
- Detecting obvious patterns that make the data meaningful

This zone is where **signal rises out of noise** — the rest of the pyramid depends on it. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## 🧠 Intermediate Transition Activities

At each boundary between levels there are key activities that shape meaning:

### 📌 Data → Information
- **Cleaning:** remove duplicates, errors  
- **Structuring:** columns, units, timestamps  
- **Contextualizing:** what does this *mean*?  
- **Filtering noise:** focus on patterns, not static

### 📌 Information → Knowledge
- **Analysis:** calculate trends, correlations  
- **Synthesis:** combine contexts to find relationships  
- **Modeling:** forming mental or computational models  
- **Interpretation:** what does this *tell us*?

### 📌 Knowledge → Wisdom
- **Judgment:** deciding what actions make sense  
- **Evaluation:** weighing trade-offs and consequences  
- **Experience:** applying pattern recognition in context  
- **Strategy:** picking a direction with foresight

These transitions show that DIKW is less “siloed stages” and more a *workflow of transformation* — which makes the funnel view useful. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## 📉 Horizontal Funnel (ASCII Visual)
```console

|---------------------- Data (lots of noise) ----------------------|
|------------------ Information ------------------|
|--------------- Knowledge -------|
|-------- Wisdom --------|
```
As data moves right, noise is shed, meaning is concentrated, and *actionable focus* emerges — because the higher levels require **less volume but more clarity** than the levels below them.

---

## 📝 Quick Reference Definitions

- **Data:** raw recordings without context — they don’t tell a story yet.  
- **Information:** organized and contextualized data giving answers to *who/what/when/where*.  
- **Knowledge:** interpretation of information, revealing *how* things relate.  
- **Wisdom:** judgment applied to knowledge — *what to do and why*.  
(Definitions adapted from common DIKW frameworks.) :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

### 📍 Notes on DIKW

The pyramid is conceptually useful but not strictly linear: many processes in analytics and decision science iterate back and forth between levels. The poster can help teams think about *workflow and transformations*, not just boxes in a graphic. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
